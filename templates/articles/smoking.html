Harmful Effects of Smoking

1. Respiratory Diseases

Smoking is the leading cause of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), which includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis. It damages lung tissues, reduces oxygen supply, and weakens the immune system, making smokers prone to infections.

2. Cardiovascular Problems

Nicotine and carbon monoxide increase heart rate, blood pressure, and the risk of stroke. Smoking also contributes to the hardening of arteries (atherosclerosis), leading to serious heart diseases.

3. Cancer Risk

Smoking is a primary cause of lung cancer and is linked to cancers of the throat, mouth, esophagus, pancreas, and bladder. The carcinogens in tobacco trigger genetic mutations, leading to abnormal cell growth.

4. Weakened Immune System

Smoking weakens the immune system, making the body more susceptible to infections. Smokers often suffer from prolonged illnesses and increased inflammation.

5. Effect on Mental Health

While nicotine initially creates a feeling of relaxation, it leads to addiction and dependency. Over time, it contributes to anxiety, depression, and cognitive decline.
