Understanding Ulcerative Colitis

Ulcerative colitis is a chronic inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) that affects the colon and rectum, causing severe discomfort, pain, and digestive issues. Patients experience symptoms like persistent diarrhea, abdominal cramps, rectal bleeding, fatigue, and unintended weight loss.

The exact cause is unknown, but it is believed to result from an immune system malfunction where the body attacks the digestive tract, leading to inflammation. Conventional treatments often involve strong medications, steroids, or even surgery, which can have significant side effects.
